# Talking Points by Execution Path
## P1 Sections - NDAA FY2026 (S.2296)

**Date:** January 2026  
**Purpose:** Structured talking points for program office and congressional staff engagement

---

## A) DEFENSE FACILITY INFRASTRUCTURE MODERNIZATION
**Sections: 2101, 2201, 2301, 2402**

### Sec. 2101 (Army Construction)
- **Authority:** Sec. 2101 authorizes Army construction projects → **Action:** Include wireless power in project specifications during design phase → **Outcome:** Battery elimination in security systems and IoT sensors, reduced maintenance costs

### Sec. 2201 (Navy Construction)
- **Authority:** Sec. 2201 authorizes Navy construction projects → **Action:** Integrate wireless power into NAVFAC facility designs → **Outcome:** Continuous power for monitoring systems, improved sensor uptime

### Sec. 2301 (Air Force Construction)
- **Authority:** Sec. 2301 authorizes Air Force construction projects → **Action:** Incorporate wireless power in AFCEC facility modernization → **Outcome:** Reduced battery maintenance burden, enhanced facility monitoring capabilities

### Sec. 2402 (ERCIP - Infrastructure Path)
- **Authority:** Sec. 2402 authorizes Energy Resilience and Conservation Investment Program funding → **Action:** Submit ERCIP application for wireless power deployment in existing facilities → **Outcome:** Energy waste reduction, improved facility resilience, measurable energy savings

---

## B) R&D PROGRAMS
**Sections: 220A, 220**

### Sec. 220A (Advanced Manufacturing)
- **Authority:** Sec. 220A authorizes advanced manufacturing program funding → **Action:** Submit SBIR/STTR Phase I proposal under advanced manufacturing topics → **Outcome:** Technology development funding, manufacturing process improvement, scale-up pathway

### Sec. 220 (Innovation Hubs)
- **Authority:** Sec. 220 authorizes dual-use innovation hub programs → **Action:** Engage DIU Commercial Solutions Opening for rapid technology demonstration → **Outcome:** Public-private partnership, technology validation, transition pathway to procurement

---

## C) ENERGY EFFICIENCY PROGRAMS
**Sections: 2402, 314**

### Sec. 2402 (ERCIP - Energy Path)
- **Authority:** Sec. 2402 authorizes ERCIP funding for energy resilience and conservation → **Action:** Submit ERCIP application emphasizing battery elimination and HVAC optimization → **Outcome:** Energy savings validated, funding for technology deployment, improved facility efficiency

### Sec. 314 (Energy Savings Reinvestment)
- **Authority:** Sec. 314 modifies energy cost savings reinvestment authority → **Action:** Use accumulated energy savings to fund wireless power deployment → **Outcome:** Self-sustaining funding mechanism, continuous technology deployment, compounding energy savings

## CROSS-CUTTING POINTS

### Battery Elimination
- **Authority:** Multiple P1 sections enable technology deployment → **Action:** Deploy wireless power to eliminate battery replacement cycles → **Outcome:** Reduced maintenance costs, eliminated battery disposal, improved operational readiness

### Sensor Uptime
- **Authority:** Infrastructure and R&D authorities support technology integration → **Action:** Provide continuous power to monitoring systems → **Outcome:** Zero downtime during power cycles, data continuity, enhanced monitoring coverage

### Energy Efficiency
- **Authority:** ERCIP and energy savings authorities fund efficiency improvements → **Action:** Deploy wireless power and air quality monitoring for HVAC optimization → **Outcome:** Measurable energy savings, reduced waste, improved facility efficiency
