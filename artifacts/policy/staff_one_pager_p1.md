# Wireless Power & Sensor Uptime: FY26 Execution Opportunity

**Date:** January 2026  
**Bill Authority:** S.2296 - NDAA FY2026  
**Priority Sections:** P1 (Immediate Action)

---

## Problem Statement

Defense facilities face three operational challenges that impact readiness and cost:

1. **Battery Maintenance Burden:** Security systems, IoT sensors, and digital displays require frequent battery replacement, creating maintenance costs and operational downtime.

2. **Sensor Downtime:** Battery-powered devices experience downtime during replacement cycles, reducing monitoring coverage and data continuity.

3. **Energy Waste:** Battery production, disposal, and replacement processes consume resources and create waste streams.

---

## Existing Authority (NDAA FY2026 - P1 Sections)

**Defense Facility Infrastructure:**
- **Sec. 2101:** Authorized Army Construction and Land Acquisition Projects
- **Sec. 2201:** Authorized Navy Construction and Land Acquisition Projects
- **Sec. 2301:** Authorized Air Force Construction and Land Acquisition Projects
- **Sec. 2402:** Authorized Energy Resilience and Conservation Investment Program (ERCIP)

**R&D Programs:**
- **Sec. 220A:** Advanced Manufacturing and Additive Manufacturing Programs
- **Sec. 220:** Dual-Use and Defense Advanced Manufacturing Innovation Hubs

**Energy Efficiency:**
- **Sec. 2402:** ERCIP (cross-cutting with infrastructure)
- **Sec. 314:** Modification of Availability and Use of Energy Cost Savings

---

## What Can Be Done This Fiscal Year

**Defense Facility Infrastructure (Sec. 2101, 2201, 2301, 2402):**
- Integrate wireless power systems into authorized MILCON projects during design phase
- Submit ERCIP funding applications for wireless power deployment in existing facilities
- Include wireless power and air quality monitoring in facility modernization specifications

**R&D Programs (Sec. 220A, 220):**
- Submit SBIR/STTR Phase I proposals under advanced manufacturing topics
- Engage DIU Commercial Solutions Opening for rapid technology demonstration
- Participate in innovation hub programs for manufacturing scale-up

**Energy Efficiency (Sec. 2402, 314):**
- Submit ERCIP applications emphasizing energy waste reduction through battery elimination
- Leverage energy cost savings reinvestment authority (Sec. 314) for technology deployment
- Demonstrate HVAC optimization through air quality monitoring integration

---

## Why Wireless Power Fits

**Battery Elimination:**
- Removes battery replacement cycles for security systems, IoT sensors, and digital displays
- Reduces maintenance labor and material costs
- Eliminates battery disposal and environmental waste

**Sensor Uptime:**
- Provides continuous power to monitoring systems
- Eliminates downtime during battery replacement
- Ensures data continuity for air quality and security monitoring

**Energy Efficiency:**
- Reduces energy waste from battery production and disposal
- Enables HVAC optimization through continuous air quality monitoring
- Supports energy resilience goals through reduced maintenance dependencies

---

## What Success Looks Like in 6-12 Months

**Defense Facility Infrastructure:**
- Wireless power integrated into at least one authorized MILCON project design
- ERCIP funding application submitted and under review
- Pilot deployment in at least one service facility demonstrating battery elimination and uptime improvement

**R&D Programs:**
- SBIR/STTR Phase I award or DIU CSO contract executed
- Technology demonstration plan approved with measurable milestones
- Partnership established with defense R&D center or innovation hub

**Energy Efficiency:**
- ERCIP funding application submitted with energy savings analysis
- Energy savings reinvestment plan approved for technology deployment
- Energy savings validated and documented in at least one facility
